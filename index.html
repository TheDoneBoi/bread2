Featured Breads

Five extraordinary breads from around the world, each telling its own story of tradition and craft.

📍 San Francisco, USA

Sourdough

The king of artisan breads. Naturally leavened with wild yeast and bacteria, sourdough develops a complex, tangy flavor and a beautifully open crumb structure over days of slow fermentation.

📍 Paris, France

Baguette

The iconic French bread — a crispy, crackled golden crust yields to a soft, airy interior. By law in France, a traditional baguette contains only flour, water, yeast, and salt.

📍 Veneto, Italy

Ciabatta

Italy's answer to the baguette. Born in 1982, this relatively modern bread features an incredibly open, hole-riddled crumb — perfect for soaking up olive oil or building the ultimate panini.

📍 Northern Europe

Dark Rye

A staple of Nordic and Eastern European baking, dark rye bread is dense, earthy, and deeply satisfying. Studded with caraway seeds, it pairs perfectly with smoked fish, butter, or sharp cheeses.

📍 Jewish Tradition

Challah

This beautifully braided, egg-enriched bread is the centerpiece of Jewish Sabbath and holiday tables. Its golden crust and pillowy-soft interior make it equally perfect for French toast the next morning.
